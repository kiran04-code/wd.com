Guiding Indian Para-athletes to Excellence since 1994

The Paralympic Committee of India (PCI) serves as the premier governing body responsible for fostering and advancing Para Sports across the nation. Recognized by the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports (MYAS), Government of India, and affiliated with the International Paralympic Committee (IPC), PCI functions as the National Sports Federation for Para Sports in India. Our primary goal is to discover, train, and support athletes, cultivating a thriving Paralympic movement and ensuring a strong presence at both national and international sporting events. With 26 State Associations, 8 Individual Para Sports Federations, and 6 National Sports Federations under its umbrella, PCI is devoted to the all-round development of Para Sports. We play a pivotal role in selecting and preparing Indian teams for the Paralympic Games and other prestigious international competitions. Our dedication has led to remarkable achievements, including an outstanding 8-medal haul at the Tokyo 2020 Paralympic Games. PCI operates in alignment with the National Sports Code 2011, reflecting our commitment to enhancing sports governance in accordance with government policies. We receive funding from the Government of India and Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) initiatives, with donations benefiting from tax exemptions, encouraging wider participation and support for our cause. In collaboration with esteemed organizations such as the Indian Army and Indian Oil Corporation Limited, PCI is actively driving initiatives to promote and expand Para Sports. Recently, in March 2024, we successfully hosted the New Delhi 2024 WSPS World Cup, demonstrating our dedication to offering world-class platforms for our athletes. With unwavering backing from the Sports Authority of India (SAI) and the Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, PCI continues to empower athletes with disabilities, enabling them to shine on the global stage.

Mission of the Paralympic Committee of India (PCI)

The Paralympic Committee of India (PCI) is dedicated to fostering the growth and excellence of Para Sports in the country. Our mission revolves around identifying, training, and supporting talented athletes with disabilities, empowering them to compete at the highest levels of national and international sports.

Core Objectives of Our Mission:

  1. Promoting Inclusivity in Sports
    • Ensuring equal opportunities for athletes with disabilities to participate and excel in various sports disciplines.
    • Encouraging widespread participation through grassroots programs and talent identification initiatives.
  2. Developing Elite Athletes
    • Providing world-class coaching, infrastructure, and resources to enhance the performance of Para athletes.
    • Preparing and selecting teams for prestigious events such as the Paralympic Games, World Championships, Asian Para Games, and other global competitions.
  3. Strengthening the Para Sports Ecosystem
    • Collaborating with State Associations, National Federations, and Government Bodies to create a structured framework for Para Sports development.
    • Aligning policies and programs with the National Sports Code 2011 and international standards.
  4. Encouraging Financial & Institutional Support
    • Securing funding from the Government of India, Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) initiatives, and public donations, ensuring sustained financial backing for Para athletes.
    • Offering scholarships, sponsorships, and employment opportunities to athletes for their long-term welfare.
  5. Hosting & Participating in Global Competitions
    • Organizing national and international championships to provide competitive exposure to Indian Para athletes.
    • Recent achievements include successfully hosting the New Delhi 2024 WSPS World Cup and securing 8 medals at the Tokyo 2020 Paralympic Games.
  6. Creating Awareness & Advocacy for Para Sports
    • Promoting disability awareness and inclusivity through media campaigns, educational programs, and community engagement.
    • Encouraging national pride and support for Para athletes by celebrating their achievements and contributions to Indian sports.

Our Story

2016

India has fielded its largest-ever delegation for the Summer Paralympic Games, with 19 competitors participating across 5 sports. The ongoing performance at the Games marks India's most successful showing in the event's history, having secured a total of 4 medals thus far ‚Äì 2 gold, 1 silver, and 1 bronze. Notably, Devendra Jhajharia set a new world record with a throw of 63.37 meters, surpassing his own previous record of 62.15 meters, to win a gold medal. Mariyappan Thangavelu achieved India's first Paralympic gold medal in Rio with a remarkable jump of 1.89 meters in the Men's T42 event. Deepa Malik made history by securing second place in the Women's Shotput F53 event, setting a personal best with a throw of 4.61 meters, becoming India's first female and oldest athlete to win a Paralympic medal. Varun Singh Bhati clinched bronze in the Men's High Jump T-42 with a personal best jump of 1.86 meters. Overall, India's performance resulted in a 43rd place finish out of 83 nations.

2012

India competed in the 2012 Summer Paralympics in London, United Kingdom, from 29 August to 9 September 2012. H.N Girisha won the silver medal in Men's High Jump F42

2009

The Paralympic Committee of India recently hosted the IWAS World Games, emphasizing the principles of unity, friendship, and sportsmanship. The event saw participation from 43 countries, with India securing the 2nd position in the medals tally. The games brought together 2100 elite athletes, 400 technical officials, and featured 250 gold medals, 200 silver medals, and 180 bronze medals. Furthermore, the event involved 300 momentums, and 500 volunteers including NCC/Scouts and Para-Army members, supported by 100 specially trained officials. This successful event underscored the organization's steadfast commitment to empowering and providing competitive opportunities to athletes with physical disabilities worldwide.

2004

Summer Paralympics In Athens When Devendra Jhajharia, Javelin Thrower, Won Gold And Rajinder Singh Won Bronze For Powerlifting In The 56-Kg Category. India Finished 53rd Out Of The 136 Nation Field
