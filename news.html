Arunachal’s Techi Sonu Selected For Sharjah 2025 International Para Athletics Championship

Jan 27, In a historic achievement for Arunachal Pradesh, para-athlete Techi Sonu has been selected by the Paralympic Committee of India (PCI) to represent India at the prestigious Sharjah 2025 International Para Athletics Championship. The championship, hosted by Althiqah Club for the Disabled, will be held from February 2 to February 4 in Sharjah, United Arab Emirates. Read More

Padma Awards 2025: Paris Paralympics gold medallist Harvinder Singh to be honoured with Padma Shri

New Delhi [India], January 25 (ANI): On the eve of the Republic Day, India's para-archer Harvinder Singh is among the recipients of the Padma Awards 2025. The Paris Paralympics gold medallist is being awarded with Padma Shri. Read More

From Setting New Benchmarks To Promising To Redefine Them, India Shattered Records At Paris 2024 Paralympics

India's para-athletes shattered the record books in Paris by delivering a performance of a lifetime, redefining the standard and raising the bar in the para-sports that will be seen as the benchmark in the coming years. Read More

Anand Mahindra offered paralympic archer Sheetal Devi artificial robotics hands. But, why did she refuse?

Paralympic archer Sheetal Devi, adopted by the Indian Army in her childhood, won a bronze medal in the mixed team compound event at the Paris Paralympics. Industrialist Anand Mahindra, who admires her determination and talent, recently met her and shared his admiration in a post on X. Sheetal gifted him an arrow, symbolizing her resilience and identity as an archer. Read More

Some Paris Olympic athletes ask for medals to be replaced after quickly deteriorating

The French mint is replacing several medals from the 2024 Paris Olympic and Paralympic Games after athletes reported deterioration. Over 100 medals are believed to be affected. The medals, designed with pieces of the Eiffel Tower, will be replaced by early 2025. The deterioration issue arose even before the Games ended, prompting complaints from multiple athletes. PCI

The Paralympic Committee of India is the apex governing body dedicated to promoting and developing Para Sport in the country. The Paralympic Committee of India spearheads the Paralympic Movement, organizes the Paralympic Games, and supports its members, empowering Para athletes to achieve excellence through dedicated training and opportunities.
